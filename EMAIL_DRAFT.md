## The problem

Every week, I receive requests from colleagues asking for help with their shoulder arthroplasty planning — reviewing implant positioning, discussing whether to use an augmented baseplate, or simply getting a second opinion before surgery. Today, these conversations happen over WhatsApp photos, screenshots, or phone calls, which makes it difficult to truly collaborate on a 3D surgical plan.

Additionally, during MasterCourse events and conversations with many Advita representatives, I have learned that a significant number of them routinely use the surgeon's own login and password to import cases and even perform the initial planning on the Equinoxe Planning App. This is a practical reality that highlights the need for a more flexible, accessible, and collaborative platform — one that does not require the desktop software to be installed on a specific machine, and that allows multiple stakeholders to participate in the planning process.

## The idea: a web-based planning platform

I have been experimenting with a concept for a **web-based version of the planning experience** — a platform that could be accessed from any device (desktop, tablet, or mobile), from anywhere, with no software installation required.

The core idea is simple: **import a case from the Equinoxe Planning App, review and modify it online, save different planning scenarios, and share it with colleagues for collaborative decision-making.**

Here is what the concept demonstrates:

- **Cross-platform access:** Works on any device with a web browser — desktop, laptop, tablet, or phone. No installation needed.
- **Case import:** Reads the existing case folders generated by the Equinoxe Planning App, including the scapula mesh, anatomical landmarks, and implant positioning data. Supports both standard EPA planning and GPS-guided cases.
- **3D visualization:** Interactive 3D viewer with the patient's scapula and baseplate, including preset views (anterior, glenoid, lateral), bone transparency, and osteophyte toggle.
- **Implant modification:** Fine controls for adjusting retroversion, inclination, depth, and translation — the same parameters available in the desktop app.
- **Multiple scenarios:** Save up to three different planning scenarios per case, allowing the surgeon to compare strategies (e.g., "standard baseplate" vs. "augmented + 2mm superior") side by side.
- **Sharing with colleagues:** Generate a shareable link that allows another surgeon to open the same case in their browser, review the 3D planning, and leave comments — without needing the Equinoxe Planning App installed.
- **Comments and collaboration:** Each shared case includes a comment section where colleagues can leave feedback, ask questions, or suggest modifications. Every action is attributed to the author for traceability.
- **Export back to desktop:** The modified planning can be exported as a .ini file compatible with the Equinoxe Planning App, closing the loop between online collaboration and the official surgical workflow.

## Why I believe this matters

The value is not in replacing the Equinoxe Planning App — it is in **extending its reach**. A web platform would allow:

1. **Surgeons** to review and adjust their own cases from any device, at any time — at home, in the office, or while traveling.
2. **Colleagues** to receive a shared link and immediately see the 3D planning, leave comments, and help with decision-making — without needing the desktop software.
3. **Representatives** to support surgeons more efficiently, without requiring access to the surgeon's personal credentials.
4. **Teams** to build a shared case library with multiple planning scenarios, creating a knowledge base for education and training.

## Important disclaimer

I want to be very clear: **this is not a product.** It is a mockup, a proof of concept, a suggestion for a possible future direction. I built it as a personal experiment to explore what a collaborative web-based planning experience could look like, and to start a conversation with the team about whether this direction could be valuable.

The prototype is functional — you can import real cases, modify the planning, save scenarios, and share links — but it is not intended for clinical use. It is simply a way to visualize and discuss the idea.
